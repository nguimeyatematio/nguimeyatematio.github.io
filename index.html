🏠 Welcome to my homepage

I am a researcher in Computational Condensed Matter and Materials Physics. My work focuses on understanding topological quantum phases in materials using Density Functional Theory (DFT) and Tight-Binding (TB) models. I study how materials with special quantum properties behave, aiming to uncover new insights into their fundamental nature.
I am also deeply interested in Quantum Computing and Quantum Machine Learning because of their potential in materials science. These tools can help us study and predict new materials and their properties in ways that go beyond classical methods. I am particularly focused on developing quantum algorithms that solve difficult problems more efficiently, using quantum computers to model complex quantum phenomena, and exploring quantum machine learning techniques that may outperform traditional approaches in analyzing and predicting complex data.

Research Interests

  • Computational Materials Science
  • Quantum Simulations
  • Quantum Algorithms

👨🏽‍💼 About Me

I am a computational condensed matter and materials physicist. I earned my PhD in Computational Condensed Matter and Materials Physics from the University of Cape Town. I also hold an MSc in Mathematical Sciences from AIMS-Cameroon, as well as a Research MSc and a BSc in Condensed Matter Physics from the University of Dschang.

My work focuses on developing accurate and efficient models to explain how materials behave at the atomic scale. I use methods such as density functional theory, tight-binding, and Wannier-based analysis to study topological behavior, phase transitions, mechanical response, and energy transport. The goal is to turn fundamental insights into practical design rules for new materials in energy and electronics.

On this website you will find my publications, short tutorials, and other resources. I am always open to collaboration and to mentoring students who are interested in computational materials research.
